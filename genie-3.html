GENIE: A Fine-Grained Measure for Novelty

Large Language Models have consistently demonstrated a lack of creativity and diversity across tasks. We propose GENIE (Granular Evaluation of Novel Ideas with Explainability) — a fine-grained evaluation metric that measures the novelty of responses along task-specific features with respect to a population of responses.
📄 Paper GitHub 🤗 HuggingFace

How GENIE works

GENIE evaluates novelty in four stages: automatic feature discovery, question generation, population building, and population-relative dissimilarity scoring.

Step 1

Discover Features

Cluster LLM-generated features/descriptions given a task.

Step 2

Generate Questions

Form and map prompt-specific questions to discovered features.

Step 3

Build Population

Define a population of responses and extract answers to the questions.

Step 4

Compute GENIE

Extract answers from a target response. The average dissimilarity between target and population answers per feature yields fine-grained novelty scores.
